On a Saturday morning in June, our team ran a free screening camp at
Karuparayan Kovil grounds, a short walk from the clinic. The goal was
simple: give the neighbourhood a low-friction way to check the three
numbers that quietly drive most long-term illness — blood sugar,
blood pressure, and body mass index.

## What we checked

- **Random blood sugar** — a finger-prick test done on the spot.
- **Blood pressure** — using calibrated digital and manual cuffs.
- **Body mass index (BMI) & waist-to-height ratio** — quick measures
  that add useful context to the sugar and BP readings.
- **Short verbal history** — recent symptoms, family history of diabetes
  or hypertension, and current medications.

Anyone whose readings landed in the borderline or high range was given
a printed note and encouraged to come in for a proper consultation, at
no cost for the initial visit.

## What we saw

A meaningful number of adults over 40 had never had their sugar or BP
checked outside of a hospital visit. Several people whose readings
were unexpectedly high had no symptoms at all — a good reminder that
early diabetes and hypertension are almost entirely silent.

> "I felt fine. I only came because my neighbour dragged me along."
> — a visitor whose blood sugar came back in the pre-diabetic range.

We also spent time on questions that come up over and over again:
what fasting sugar levels actually mean, why HbA1c matters more than
a single reading, and how to tell whether the "sweet tooth" running
in the family is normal or worth investigating.
